Presenting Forecasts to the Board: Handling Pushback and Building Trust

by Harvard Law School Forum on Corporate Governance

Effective board presentations require transparent communication, challenging assumptions rigorously, and developing multiple plausible scenarios (base, bull, bear cases). Position the board as a strategic partner by emphasizing healthy tension through objectivity and pre-setting non-negotiable priorities to address the triple constraint of time, budget, and scope.

WHAT YOU'LL LEARN → Structure your forecast presentation around 3–4 scenarios with explicit assumptions and trade-offs, and frame board skepticism as a resource rather than an obstacle.
