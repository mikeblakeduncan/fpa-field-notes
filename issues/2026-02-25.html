5 Steps to Build Rolling Forecast Models

by Phoenix Strategy Group

Structured approach to rolling forecasts: secure stakeholder buy-in, define framework (12-18 month horizon updated monthly), gather and analyze historical data, build the model, establish regular reviews with scenario analysis. The process shifts teams from annual budgets to continuous planning with clear roles and performance tracking.

WHAT YOU'LL LEARN → Rolling forecasts require explicit stakeholder alignment and a defined time horizon (e.g., 12 months rolling); implement monthly/quarterly updates with variance tracking to improve forecast accuracy over time.

Storytelling Tips for CFOs: Presenting Financial Forecasts to the Board

by OneStream

Board presentations of forecasts succeed when CFOs lead with strategic narrative and visuals rather than data dumps, tailor forecasts to business context using industry benchmarks, and build multiple scenarios (conservative, base, optimistic). Pre-sharing materials and rehearsing for challenges significantly reduces friction.

WHAT YOU'LL LEARN → Prepare forecast presentations with multiple scenarios, pre-share slides, and lead with business context and storytelling—not raw numbers.
