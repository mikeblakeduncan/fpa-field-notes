Board Forecast Presentation: Visual Storytelling and Multi-Scenario Planning

by Datasite

Pre-wiring board presentations by talking to directors 48-72 hours in advance surfaces concerns early, letting you address them asynchronously rather than in the meeting itself. Keep presentations to 12-15 slides, frame forecasts as strategic decisions (not updates), and specify upfront whether you're seeking approval, input, or acknowledgment.

WHAT YOU'LL LEARN → Structure board forecast presentations around a clear decision or outcome rather than information dump, and pre-wire key stakeholders to surface objections before the meeting.

Managing Difficult Conversations with Business Partners: Preparation and Collaborative Framing

by ContentStack

Difficult financial conversations succeed when you pinpoint the core issue (costs, revenue loss, competitive threat), align on mutual outcomes upfront, and choose in-person or video format rather than email. Anticipate CFO priorities around financial logic and create psychological safety by asking clarifying questions and actively listening.

WHAT YOU'LL LEARN → Before a tough conversation with a business partner about budget or spending, identify the root financial problem, define what 'win' looks like for both sides, and hold it live to read tone and build collaborative momentum.
